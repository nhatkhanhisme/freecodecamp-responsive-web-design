Ho Chi Minh

The great leader of the Vietnamese nation

Here's a time line of Ho Chi Minh life:

  • May 19, 1890: Ho Chi Minh was born in Kim Lien Village, Nam Dan District, Nghe An Province, Vietnam, under the name Nguyen Sinh Cung
  • 1907–1908: He took the name Nguyen Tat Thanh and studied at Quoc Hoc Hue High School for the Gifted
  • 1910: While traveling to Saigon, he stopped in Phan Thiet to work as a schoolteacher at Dục Thanh School
  • June 5, 1911: Departed from Nha Rong Wharf aboard the ship Amiral Latouche-Tréville, beginning his overseas journey in search of a way to save Vietnam
  • 1919: Submitted the "Eight-Point Petition of the Annamese People" to the Versailles Peace Conference, using the name Nguyen Ai Quoc (Nguyen the Patriot)
  • 1920: Joined the French Communist Party at the Tours Congress, adopting Marxist-Leninist ideologies to guide Vietnam's struggle for independence
  • 1924: Attended the Fifth Congress of the Communist International and engaged in revolutionary activities in the Soviet Union and other countries
  • 1930: Founded the Communist Party of Vietnam while in Hong Kong
  • 1941: Returned to Vietnam to lead the Viet Minh (League for the Independence of Vietnam) in the fight against colonial forces
  • September 2, 1945: Proclaimed Vietnam's independence at Ba Dinh Square, founding the Democratic Republic of Vietnam. He served as President of Vietnam from 1945 to 1969
  • 1946–1954: Led resistance efforts against French colonial forces, culminating in the decisive victory at Dien Bien Phu
  • 1954–1969: Played a key role in guiding North Vietnam during the Vietnam War and led efforts to unify the country under a communist framework.
  • September 2, 1969: Passed away in Hanoi, leaving behind a historical testament advocating for a peaceful, independent, united, and prosperous Vietnam
